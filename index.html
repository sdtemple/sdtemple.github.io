About

I am a PhD student in the Department of Statistics at the University of Washington. I am interested in modeling biological systems as stochastic processes. This interest motivates my participation in the Browning Lab, in the UW Statistical Genetics Seminar, and in the Summer Institute in Statistical Genetics. For Summer 2020, I am building Bayesian dynamic occupancy models for viruses in mosquito populations at Los Alamos National Laboratory.

Before coming to UW, I provided actuarial services to Liberty Mutual. I completed my undergraduate studies at University of Oregon where I majored in mathematics and minored in computer and information sciences.
